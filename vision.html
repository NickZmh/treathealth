Imagine a world where technology is at the forefornts of improving care for the underserved.

Where Health Isn’t Just Limited To A Doctor’s Visit

Medical care only has a 10% impact on life expectancy, while 40% of life expectancy is impacted by individual behavior and 20% by social and environmental factors.* It's clear that your zip-code and behavior are the primary drivers of your wellbeing. We believe in rewiring our members to take control of their health through proper education, support and technology.

*Source: Kaiser Foundation: "Beyond Health Care: The Role of Social Determinants in Promoting Health and Health Equity" 2018

Where Interoperability Has No Barriers

Health care is very fragmented and many of its components are alientated. There’s clear documented gaps in health information technology‘s (HIT) lack of asynchronous communication among other mechanisms. This unequivocally has an impact on overall care coordination.

We're rigorously designing HIT to improve care coordination through sustainable and scalable models across information transfer, monitoring of patients, data transparency, real-time engagement and triggers, tools supporting self-management goals, links with caregivers and community resources, etc.

Where All Your Health Needs Are A Click Away

With cell-phone gaps substantially narrowing between low-income (Medicaid) and the middle-class, we know it's inevitable that your cell-phone will be your access to a plethora of wonders, so why not healthcare? We're centralizing health care for our members, and that means that they won't have to scour around multiple health apps for basic resources such as booking, chatting, asking, planning and more. Simultaneously, we're also integrating our services with other vendors.
